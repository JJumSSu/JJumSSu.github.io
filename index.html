About

Hello! I am a Ph.D. student in the School of Interactive Computing at Georgia Tech advised by Alan Ritter. I formerly worked as an applied scientist at Naver Clova, where I actively shaped the development of a foundational vision-language model: Hyperclova-X-Vision. Prior to Naver, I received B.E. in Statistics, B.S, and M.E. in Computer Science & Engineering from Korea University.

My research pursuits aim to create a comprehensive multimodal system capable of perceiving, comprehending, and reasoning. To achieve this goal, my research interests include aligning language and vision modalities and enhancing model comprehension of complex structural visual information. Building on this development, I am interested in applying such a multimodal system to expedite scientific discoveries.
